Project

ULTRANET is a multi-channel audiovisual installation co-created with Ryka Ali, translating the overstimulating architecture of online media consumption into a physical gallery environment.

Tools & Skills
  • ▸ TouchDesigner — audio-reactive system
  • ▸ FL Studio — original score composition
  • ▸ Adobe Creative Suite — collage & branding
  • ▸ Systems Thinking — 10-projector AV setup
  • ▸ Video Production — 50 source collages
  • ▸ Brand & Social Strategy — daily post schedule
Collaborator: Ryka Ali

Challenge

The core challenge was translating the disorienting, dopamine-driven logic of doomscrolling into a spatial, sensory experience — without simply replicating a screen.

Research drew on the San Francisco Video Riots of the late 1990s (live multi-projection events) and MK Ultra's psychological manipulation experiments, establishing a framework that treats surveillance capitalism, influencer culture, and cognitive overload as interconnected conditions of digital life.

Practically: producing enough content to cover every wall of the gallery — 50 video collages sourced from 200+ found-footage clips — and synchronizing them to an original hour-long music set via a live TouchDesigner audio-reactive pipeline. The installation required mounting 10 ceiling projectors connected by 50-foot HDMI cables across borrowed hardware from multiple contributors.

Outcome

Hundreds of attendees over 4 hours. The event was free — a deliberate decision to preserve democratic access to the work and remain consistent with its critique of attention-economy platforms.

The intended effect was contemplative and unsettling. What materialized was closer to a party — an unintentional but conceptually resonant outcome: the same apathy and dissociation the work diagnosed played out live in the room.

The project demonstrated end-to-end production ownership: concept development, archival research, video editing, music composition, real-time AV systems, event logistics, and public-facing brand strategy — all executed under one cohesive conceptual framework.

Key Takeaway: ULTRANET proved the ability to carry a conceptually rigorous idea from initial research through full-scale physical installation — adapting in real time, leading cross-functional collaboration, and making decisive creative choices under constraint.
