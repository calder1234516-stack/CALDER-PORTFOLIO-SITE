Are You Coming
to the Party?

publication  ·  2024

Project

Are You Coming to the Party? is a 156-page documentary publication co-created with Ryka Ali, capturing the underground nightlife scene of New York City — its promoters, DJs, bottle girls, party-goers, and the self-organized spaces that define the city's most compelling after-dark culture.

The publication is positioned against nostalgia and mainstream club documentation: it centers emerging voices and smaller, community-driven events over established institutions, building a vernacular editorial language rooted in authenticity rather than spectacle.

Collaborator: Ryka Ali

Challenge

The central challenge was documenting NYC nightlife in a way that felt authentic — not nostalgic, not judgmental. Nightlife photography has a long history of either glamorizing or othering its subjects; the project required developing an editorial framework that could hold space for intimacy, critique, and community without collapsing into either.

Research spanned 25 years of party photography, underground zines, and nightlife archives. Field research ran parallel: identifying the most interesting people within their immediate network to interview and photograph. The editorial subjects — a promoter, two DJs, and a bottle girl — were chosen deliberately to represent the full labor ecosystem of a party, not just its visible performers.

The final production phase compressed into a hard deadline, with editing and layout running until the night before submission. Working under sustained pressure required fast decision-making, real-time prioritization, and maintaining design quality without the buffer of revision cycles.

Outcome

The finished publication functions as both a cultural time capsule and a proof of editorial vision. It successfully documented emerging voices in NYC nightlife while building a coherent, community-rooted identity across 156 pages — designed, written, and produced collaboratively from the ground up.

While AYCTP was not commercialized, it directly informed the production and business infrastructure built for LOGIN Magazine — serving as a prototype for how to execute an independent publication at scale. The lessons in what not to do (distribution, monetization, audience building) became the blueprint for LOGIN's successful pre-order and profitable first release.

Key Takeaway: AYCTP demonstrated the capacity to lead a high-stakes, deadline-driven editorial production — building skills in creative direction, community documentation, and collaborative design that directly enabled the commercial success of LOGIN.
