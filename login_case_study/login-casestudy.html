OVERVIEW

CS-001/OVERVIEW

LOGIN is a 134-page independent print publication documenting digital life, art, and culture — specifically the artists, designers, and technologists whose practice is shaped by the contemporary internet: surveillance capitalism, AI, and the institutions of late-stage social media.

The magazine positions itself within a lineage: net art (the 90s/00s experimentation with a new medium), Post-Internet art (the 2010s critique of big data and attention economy), and the current moment — what comes after the systems we inherited from 25 years of platform capitalism.

TOOLS & SKILLS
▸ Editorial Design — 134-page InDesign layout
▸ Branding — identity, visual language, tone
▸ Print Production — prepress, bulk print run
▸ Entrepreneurship — pre-order, profitable release
▸ PR & Marketing — social strategy, daily cadence
▸ Operations — logistics, fulfillment, customer comms
▸ Interviewing & Writing — 7 original interviews + op-eds

CHALLENGE
The central challenge was building every layer of an independent magazine from scratch — alone. Concept, editorial vision, interview sourcing, writing, layout, production, marketing, and fulfillment all ran concurrently with no team.

Editorially, the task was identifying the right voices: people actively working at the edge of the internet's current transition. Interviews included Riley Daggs (AI for medical research), Jake Harper (SOOT — a new image search interface), Aidan Walker (internet culture researcher and writer), and Zane Kind (Silk — a blogging platform subverting the social media business model). Each required individual outreach, interview editing, and section layout.

Beyond editorial, LOGIN had to function as a business. The pre-order phase needed to fund the print run, which required building marketing infrastructure and public-facing brand trust from zero — without institutional backing or a budget.

OUTCOME
The first release turned a profit. What began as a school project became a functioning independent publication with a paying readership, a distributed print run, and an ongoing editorial identity.

The pre-order campaign funded the bulk print run in full. The remaining inventory supports a launch event and potential consignment placement — expanding distribution beyond the initial audience.

LOGIN demonstrated that a single designer, operating with full creative control, can produce and commercialize a print publication at professional scale. It strengthened cross-disciplinary execution: design, writing, production, PR, and business operations — all within one sustained project.

KEY TAKEAWAY
LOGIN proves independent publishing is viable as both a cultural project and a business — and demonstrates the capacity to own every phase of a complex, multi-disciplinary production from first concept through profitable release.
